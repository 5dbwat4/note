# 27-1 Introduction: Fundamental Law of Electrostatics(静电学)

- Coulomb’s Law: Force between two point charges
- Gauss’ Law: Relationship between Electric Fields and charges
- Although Gauss’ law and Coulomb’s law give the identical results.
- Gauss’ Law offers a much simpler way to calculate E in situations with a high degree of symmetry (高对称的情况).
- Gauss’ Law as the fundamental law of electrostatics.
- Gauss’ Law is valid in the case of rapidly moving charges.
- Gauss’ Law is more general than Coulomb’s law.

# A Closed Surface (闭合曲面)

## Flux Calculation Through a Closed Surface

Surface Elements and Their Flux

For each element of the closed surface, the flux is calculated as follows:

$$ \Phi_{1}=\vec{v} \cdot \vec{A}_{1}=-v A_{1} $$

$$ \Phi_{2}=\vec{v} \cdot \vec{A}_{2}=0 $$

$$ \Phi_{3}=\vec{v} \cdot \vec{A}_{3}=v A_{3} \cos \theta $$

$$ \Phi_{4}=\vec{v} \cdot \vec{A}_{4}=0 $$

$$ \Phi_{5}=\vec{v} \cdot \vec{A}_{5}=0 $$

Total Net Flux

The total flux through the closed surface is the sum of all individual fluxes:

$$ \Phi=\Phi_{1}+\Phi_{2}+\Phi_{3}+\Phi_{4}+\Phi_{5}=-v A_{1}+v A_{3} \cos \theta $$

Key Physical Principle

**For incompressible fluid in steady flow through a closed surface:**
$$ \Phi=\sum\vec{v} \cdot \vec{A} = 0 $$


For any closed surface, the net flux of an incompressible fluid in steady flow is zero.


$$
\Phi = \oiint \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0
$$


如果在所述体积内存在一个流体的**源**（Source），则意味着有流体从体积内部产生并向外流出。

此时，通过闭合曲面的**净通量大于零**：

$$
\Phi = \oiint\limits_{S} \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{A} > 0
$$

> **物理意义**：有净流体从闭合曲面内部流出。

体积内存在汇 (Sink) 的情况

如果在所述体积内存在一个流体的**汇**，则意味着流体在体积内部被吸收或消失。

此时，通过闭合曲面的**净通量小于零**：

$$
\Phi = \oiint\limits_{S} \vec{v} \cdot d\vec{A} < 0
$$

> **物理意义**：有净流体流入闭合曲面内部。



# 2. The Flux of the Electric Field (电通量)

> Even although nothing is flowing in the electrostatic case, we still use the concept of field.

Let's quantify previous discussion about field-line "counting".

Definition: Electric Flux ($\Phi_E$)

The electric flux $\Phi_E$ through the closed surface A is defined as:

**Discrete form (for small area elements):**
$$
\Phi_{E} = \sum \vec{E} \cdot \vec{A}
$$

**Continuous form (integral form):**
$$
\Phi_{E} = \oiint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A}
$$

## Physical Interpretation

- **The number of lines of electric field (电力线数目) passing through the closed surface A.**
- Represents the "flow" of electric field through a surface, even though nothing is physically flowing in electrostatic situations.
